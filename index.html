Proposal Microsite / IG Persona Strategy

01

The Opportunity

Insurance communication often becomes too sales-driven, too abstract, or too visibly persuasive.

For modern adults, especially 35+, the barrier is rarely awareness. It is emotional distance. Traditional messaging tends to feel like conversion-first communication rather than something grounded in real life, real priorities, and real personal decision-making.

The opportunity for Blue is to shift the framing: from “insurance as a product being sold” to “planning as a normal, intelligent, self-respecting part of adulthood.” Instagram then becomes not a hard-sell funnel, but a content world where trust is built through tone, credibility, and relevance.

02

Why This Makes Sense for Blue

The proposal aligns with Blue’s operating principles rather than forcing an artificial content persona onto the brand.

Digital-first

The format suits a brand already positioned around modern, simplified digital behaviour.

Simple

The content tone reduces jargon and reframes protection in practical, everyday language.

Flexible

A persona-led system allows Blue to scale content across education, reassurance, and product relevance.

Transparent

The messaging can feel open and calm, rather than scripted around urgency or pressure.

No commission

This gives Blue a distinct trust advantage and supports a lower-pressure, advisory content voice.

Low-friction experience

The Instagram-to-site journey can feel seamless, modern, and aligned with the brand promise.

03

Audience Reframing

This should not be approached as a youth-trend content play.

The strongest opportunity is with modern adults aged 35+, people who are already balancing career identity, family or future-family responsibilities, long-term financial planning, and a desire for more intentional living. This audience does not need hyper-youthful energy. They respond better to clarity, credibility, measured aspiration, and emotionally intelligent content that respects their stage of life.

35+ modern adults planning mindset IG-native but not trend-led trust-sensitive

Recommended Direction

The strongest balance of relevance, brand fit, and Instagram viability is Vanessa Leung.

Vanessa gives Blue a clearer distinction from both traditional insurance advertising and younger influencer culture. She feels believable, self-possessed, modern, and appropriately aspirational without becoming flashy. Most importantly, she creates a natural bridge between Blue’s brand values and the emotional reality of long-term financial planning.

06

Content World

The chosen persona should generate a coherent Instagram ecosystem, not isolated promotional posts.

Life planning signals

Content about routines, priorities, decision-making, and what a well-structured adult life actually looks like.

Money mindset without jargon

Calm, digestible reflections on savings discipline, protection, future-readiness, and practical confidence.

Soft education

Simple content that explains concepts without sounding like a financial lecture or sales presentation.

Day-in-context storytelling

Moments from work, planning, home, and everyday decisions that make the persona feel lived-in and credible.

07

Brand Integration

Blue should appear as a natural enabler of confidence and clarity, not as a hard interruption.

The brand is best integrated through relevance: moments when planning, protection, or future decisions genuinely arise in the persona’s content world. Blue becomes present as a modern, low-friction option that supports how she already thinks and lives.

This allows the product story — especially the 100K saving plan and life insurance offering — to enter the narrative with less resistance. The result is softer brand acceptance, stronger trust formation, and a more premium content feel.
