02

Market Context

Before proposing the answer, the page should establish why this model makes sense within the category and within current audience behaviour.

Hong Kong is a mature, high-value, heavily intermediated insurance market — but much of the category’s communication still feels corporate, product-led, and socially distant. That creates room for a brand like Blue to differentiate through identity, tone, and social relevance rather than information publishing alone.

Category behaviour

Most insurance brands still communicate like institutions first and social brands second.

Audience behaviour

Familiarity and trust are often built more naturally through recognisable personalities than through product-first brand broadcasting.

Strategic implication

The next advantage for Blue is not more content alone, but a more memorable and more socially ownable communication system.

Build a trust-led digital persona system that makes Blue feel more human, more relevant, and easier to choose over time.

Platform Logic

Instagram and Threads should not do the same job. Their roles should be clear and disciplined.

Instagram

The main visual and storytelling platform, where persona identity, content world, and aspiration become visible and emotionally resonant.

Threads

A lighter conversational layer for relevance, point of view, and everyday social familiarity around the persona.

Together, the two platforms create a more complete communication system than either one can alone.
