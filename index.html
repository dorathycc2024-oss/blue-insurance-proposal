01

The Opportunity

Insurance communication often struggles not because the category lacks importance, but because the communication model feels too product-led, too corporate, and too socially detached.

For modern audiences, especially 35+, trust is not built through urgency alone. It is built through relevance, credibility, and the sense that a brand understands how financial planning fits into real life rather than interrupting it.

The opportunity for Blue is to shift from a purely product-first communication style to a more persona-led social model, where protection, savings, and future planning are introduced through context, life stage, and social familiarity.

03

Why This Makes Sense for Blue

This approach is not about making insurance trendy. It is about making the brand more understandable, more relatable, and more appropriate to the platform.

For Blue, the challenge is not only category awareness. It is whether savings and life insurance can be introduced in a way that feels relevant and credible inside social environments where audiences respond more strongly to human context than transactional messaging.

By shifting from a product-first communication style to a persona-led social model, Blue can position savings and protection as part of a broader conversation about planning well, living responsibly, and preparing for the future.

This is particularly important for a 35+ audience, where major decisions are shaped less by urgency and more by maturity, trust, and the desire to make thoughtful long-term choices.

05

Key Strategic Message

This is not a virtual influencer gimmick. It is a communication model.

The role of the persona is to build trust, relevance, and social credibility first. Product connection comes later, once the audience has context, familiarity, and reason to care. The objective is not to create a fictional celebrity; it is to create a social communication system that makes Blue more platform-appropriate and more persuasive over time.
