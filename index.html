01

Opportunity

Traditional insurance communication often feels too sales-led, too abstract, and too detached from lived reality.

For modern adults, especially those aged 35+, the challenge is not only awareness. It is whether the brand feels relevant, trustworthy, and socially appropriate within a platform environment shaped by lifestyle, identity, and everyday context.

The opportunity for Blue is to move away from communication that feels overly corporate, transactional, or purely product-led, and instead create a more human Instagram presence where financial planning feels naturally connected to real life stages, priorities, and aspirations.

03

Why This Makes Sense for Blue

For Blue, this approach makes sense because the category challenge is not only about awareness — it is also about relevance, trust, and how financial products are introduced in a social environment.

On Instagram, audiences are less responsive to communication that feels overly corporate, product-led, or transactional. What performs better is content that feels human, context-driven, and naturally connected to real life stages, priorities, and aspirations.

By shifting from a purely product-first approach to a more lifestyle-led and persona-informed content strategy, Blue can build stronger emotional relevance while still supporting long-term business goals. Rather than treating savings and protection as isolated product messages, the brand can position them as part of a broader conversation around planning well, living responsibly, and preparing for the future.

This is especially important for a 35+ audience, where decisions around savings and life insurance are rarely driven by urgency alone. More often, they are shaped by maturity, trust, and the desire to make thoughtful long-term choices. A more human Instagram presence allows Blue to meet that audience in a way that feels more credible, more contemporary, and more aligned with how people already consume content today.

This is not about making insurance look trendy — it is about making the brand feel more understandable, more relatable, and more appropriate to the platform.

05

12-Month Strategy / One-Year Roadmap

This proposal is not only a concept direction. It is designed to support a full-year operating model with milestone-based growth, clear posting cadence, and long-term brand relevance.

Months 1–3

Foundation and Brand Reset

Milestone: Establish a clearer Instagram identity for Blue and build the foundation for trust.

In the first three months, the focus is on resetting Blue’s Instagram presence with a more mature, lifestyle-aware, and audience-relevant communication style. Content should begin introducing the new brand tone, clarify the role of savings and protection in everyday life, and create a more visually consistent and credible feed.

Monthly content output: 3–5 Reels + 4–5 image posts

Total: 7–10 posts per month

Expected outcome: A stronger brand foundation, clearer social identity, and improved relevance for a 35+ audience.

Months 4–6

Relevance and Audience Connection

Milestone: Build stronger audience resonance through life-stage and persona-led storytelling.

In this phase, content should deepen relevance by focusing on the real concerns, aspirations, and decision-making moments that shape financial planning in adult life. This is where Blue begins to feel more emotionally connected to its audience, moving beyond product awareness into everyday relevance.

Monthly content output: 3–5 Reels + 4–5 image posts

Total: 7–10 posts per month

Expected outcome: Stronger engagement, more recognisable content direction, and increased audience familiarity with Blue’s positioning.

Months 7–9

Consideration and Product Connection

Milestone: Connect audience interest more directly to Blue’s savings and protection solutions.

Once the brand voice and audience relationship are more established, content can begin linking more clearly to practical planning needs. This is the stage where the 100K saving plan and life insurance offering should appear more directly within the content ecosystem — but still through soft, contextual, and lifestyle-aware storytelling rather than hard selling.

Monthly content output: 3–5 Reels + 4–5 image posts

Total: 7–10 posts per month

Expected outcome: Higher consideration value, clearer association between content and product relevance, and improved audience readiness for inquiry.

Months 10–12

Long-Term Brand Equity and Growth

Milestone: Position Blue as a trusted long-term presence and work toward scalable follower growth.

In the final stage, the goal is to reinforce consistency, trust, and brand memorability. Blue should by now feel less like a traditional insurance brand and more like a credible, mature presence within the audience’s long-term planning journey. Content should continue supporting relevance and trust while strengthening the account’s visibility and growth momentum toward the 20,000-follower annual target.

Monthly content output: 3–5 Reels + 4–5 image posts

Total: 7–10 posts per month

Expected outcome: A more established Instagram presence, stronger long-term brand value, and measurable progress toward 20K followers within 12 months.

06

Content Approach / Content Pillars

To make Blue more relevant on Instagram, content should move beyond product explanation alone and instead build a more human, lifestyle-aware brand presence.

The goal is not to remove financial credibility, but to express it through content that feels more socially natural, emotionally resonant, and aligned with the lives of a 35+ audience.

This means Blue’s content approach should balance three functions: building trust through a mature and credible tone, increasing relevance through life-stage and lifestyle-led storytelling, and creating consideration by gradually connecting everyday concerns to savings, protection, and long-term planning.

Rather than speaking only at the moment of purchase, Blue should build an ongoing content ecosystem that helps the audience see financial planning as part of a well-lived and well-prepared life.

A successful Instagram presence for Blue should not only explain products — it should create a content environment where savings and protection feel naturally relevant to everyday life.
