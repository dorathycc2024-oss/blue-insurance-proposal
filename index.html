Strategic Statement

The role of the persona is to build trust, relevance, and social credibility first — so Blue can enter the conversation in a more human, socially appropriate, and commercially persuasive way.

01

The Opportunity

Insurance communication often underperforms not because the category lacks importance, but because the communication model feels too product-led, too corporate, and too socially detached.

Key Observation

For modern 35+ audiences, trust is built through relevance before conversion.

Blue’s opportunity is not only to explain products more clearly. It is to create a social communication model where savings and protection feel naturally connected to real life stages, priorities, and aspirations.

That means moving away from communication that feels overly corporate or transactional, and toward a persona-led system that is more socially credible, more human, and more platform-appropriate.

03

Why This Can Work for Blue

This is a communication model, not just a character concept.

Blue’s opportunity is to feel more human and more socially credible while still remaining commercially grounded.

A persona-led communication model helps Blue speak more naturally about savings, planning, and protection by rooting those ideas in life stage, mindset, and social context.

This is especially relevant for mature audiences, where financial decisions are shaped by trust, long-term thinking, and the desire to make responsible choices — not only by urgency.
